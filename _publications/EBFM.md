We propose a novel empirical Bayes fine-mapping framework (EBFM) that accurately quantifies the causal probability for genetic variants by integrating Genome-wide Association Studies (GWAS) summary statistics with linkage disequilibrium (LD) information. Unlike existing methods that rely on less accurate variational approximations or generate redundant credible sets, EBFM leverages a Gibbs-sampling-based algorithm combined with a greedy search strategy to efficiently estimate posterior inclusion probabilities and construct minimal yet informative credible sets. Simulation studies demonstrate that EBFM significantly improves power and coverage in identifying true causal variants compared to existing state-of-the-art approaches. Furthermore, real-data analysis on body mass index (BMI) across diverse populations confirms the robustness and replicability of EBFM in resolving complex polygenic traits.
